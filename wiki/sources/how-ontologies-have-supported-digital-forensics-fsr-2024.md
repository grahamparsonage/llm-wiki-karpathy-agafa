## Problem and aim

Digital forensics depends on shared vocabulary for devices, traces, processes, and legal contexts; ontologies clarify knowledge structure and enable sharing, but heterogeneity of formats and domain specialization makes ontology work challenging. Prior work addressed isolated DF subtopics (for example email forensics or privacy-preserving DF); this SLR synthesizes ontology practices **across DF more broadly**.

## Methodology (from abstract and introduction)

- **Design:** Systematic literature review over primary studies reporting DF ontologies.
- **Corpus:** Analysis of **29 ontologies** to answer **six research questions** (SRQs).
- **DF process framing:** The authors adopt main DF phases described as **identification, collection, organization, and presentation (reporting)** when relating ontology support to investigative workflow.

## Themes from the introduction (confirmed in excerpt)

- Ontologies clarify domain structure and underpin knowledge-based systems; challenges include agreeing on standard vocabularies, encoding expert knowledge, choosing representation languages, and modeling classes and attributes rigorously.
- DF ontologies appear across **tools/software**, **evidence-analysis techniques and standards**, and **process-oriented** DF descriptions.
