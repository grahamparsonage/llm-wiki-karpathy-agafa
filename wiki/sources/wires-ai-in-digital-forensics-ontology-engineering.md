Review article arguing that ontology engineering is a practical path to partial automation in digital forensics by enabling semantic integration, querying, and automated reasoning.

## Citation
Sikos, L. F. (2021). *AI in digital forensics: Ontology engineering for cybercrime investigations*. WIREs Forensic Science, 3:e1394. https://doi.org/10.1002/wfs2.1394

## Key points
- Digital forensics faces volume, variety, and heterogeneity challenges that limit manual workflows.
- Taxonomies alone are not enough for advanced automation; expressive ontologies are needed.
- Semantic Web standards (RDF, RDFS, OWL, SWRL, SPARQL) support structured representation, reasoning, and query-driven analysis.
- Ontology-based approaches improve data fusion, consistency checking, anomaly detection, and timeline reconstruction.
- Chain-of-custody and admissibility concepts can be represented explicitly for machine-assisted validation.
- Major constraints remain: limited standardization, proprietary ontology silos, and difficulty converting unstructured evidence into structured semantic data.
