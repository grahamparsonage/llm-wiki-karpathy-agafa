Paper proposing SCOPE, a smart-city-focused ontology extension to UCO/CASE for representing threats, cybercrime, investigation workflows, and forensic evidence in cyber-physical environments.

## Citation

Tok, Y. C., Zheng, D. Y., & Chattopadhyay, S. (2025). A Smart City Infrastructure Ontology for Threats, Cybercrime, and Digital Forensic Investigation. arXiv:2408.02023v2.

## Problem and aim

Existing digital-forensics ontologies (including UCO/CASE) support interoperability but do not provide enough smart-city infrastructure context for investigation-heavy workflows. The paper introduces SCOPE to bridge that gap.

## Core contributions

- Defines a modular ontology profile (SCOPE) extending UCO/CASE for smart-city investigations.
- Integrates threat models, forensic evidence structures, and workflow-relevant entities for SCI environments.
- Aligns representation with MITRE ATT&CK and CAPEC terminology to support defender-investigator coordination.
- Demonstrates usage through a realistic incident scenario modeled on publicly reported APT-like incidents.

## Key implications

- Reduces ad hoc data modeling burden for investigators in emerging cyber-physical contexts.
- Improves sharing of structured investigation data across tools and teams.
- Supports lifecycle linkage among threats, attack techniques, evidence artifacts, and response workflows.

## Relevance to this wiki

- Extends current ontology/forensics coverage from classic DF contexts toward smart-city cyber-physical systems.
- Useful for future AGAFA-related planning where domain-specific ontology extensions are needed.
- Strengthens the wiki's emphasis on interoperability, explainability, and evidence traceability.
