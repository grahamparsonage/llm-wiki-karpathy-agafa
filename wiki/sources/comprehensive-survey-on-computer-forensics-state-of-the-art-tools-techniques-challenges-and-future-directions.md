Broad survey of computer forensics domains and tooling, including feature-oriented comparisons intended to support practical forensic tool selection.

## Citation

Javed, A. R., Ahmed, W., Alazab, M., Jalil, Z., Kifayat, K., & Gadekallu, T. R. (2022). A comprehensive survey on computer forensics: State-of-the-art, tools, techniques, challenges, and future directions. *IEEE Access*, 10, 11065-11102. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2022.3142508

## Problem and aim

Rapid growth of cybercrime increases demand for reliable digital investigation practice, but investigators face a fragmented tooling ecosystem and diverse evidence sources. The survey aims to map current domains, tool capabilities, research gaps, and future directions.

## Scope covered in the paper

- Computer forensic domains including operating-system, file-system, live-memory, web, email, network, and multimedia forensics.
- Open-source and proprietary forensic toolkits, with cross-feature comparison to support investigator decision-making.
- Discussion of practical constraints and emerging challenges from modern platforms and distributed environments.

## Key findings

- Tool choice should be case-driven and feature-driven; no single toolkit is optimal for every scenario.
- Cross-platform support, multi-filesystem handling, automation/scripting extensibility, and ecosystem support are recurring selection factors.
- Emerging environments (for example cloud/virtualized/distributed systems) continue to widen evidence complexity and process burden.
