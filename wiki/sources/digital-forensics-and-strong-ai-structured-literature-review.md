Structured review assessing how strong-AI framing appears in digital forensics literature and where evidence remains limited.

## Citation
Fahndrich, J., Honekamp, W., Povalej, R., Rittelmeier, H., Berner, S., & Labudde, D. (2023). *Digital forensics and strong AI: A structured literature review*. Forensic Science International: Digital Investigation, 46, 301617. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fsidi.2023.301617

## Key points
- Reviews literature at the intersection of digital forensics and strong-AI framing, clarifying current maturity boundaries.
- Finds most DF AI work remains narrow-task and weak-AI oriented despite broad strong-AI rhetoric in some discourse.
- Highlights governance, accountability, and explainability constraints as central blockers for stronger autonomy claims.
- Supports cautious deployment posture with human accountability and method-level validation evidence.
