Paper presenting CASE as a community-developed cyber-investigation specification language aligned with and extending UCO, evolving from DFAX toward broader interoperable use.

## Citation

Casey, E., Barnum, S., Griffith, R., Snyder, J., van Beek, H., & Nelson, A. (2017). Advancing coordinated cyber-investigations and tool interoperability using a community developed specification language. *Digital Investigation*, 22, 14-45.

## Problem and aim

Cyber-investigations often combine data from many tools, organizations, and jurisdictions, but existing exchange formats are insufficient for normalized cross-tool analysis and provenance-centric workflows. The paper develops CASE to improve harmonized representation and sharing.

## Core contributions

- Defines CASE as an open, community-developed specification for cyber-investigation information exchange.
- Aligns CASE to UCO as a shared ontology backbone for cross-domain cyber concepts.
- Positions CASE as a flexible progression from DFAX with wider context coverage (criminal, corporate, intelligence).
- Demonstrates proof-of-concept tooling and structured serialization to support interoperability and validation.

## Key implications

- Reduces data-friction in multi-tool investigations by enabling normalization and machine-assisted correlation.
- Supports representation of handling, transfer, processing, analysis, and interpretation details alongside technical traces.
- Strengthens provenance and data-marking support for trust/classification-aware sharing.

## Relevance to this wiki

- Critical primary source for CASE/UCO/DFAX lineage and design intent.
- Improves evidence quality for implementation-readiness comparisons among ontology/specification stacks.
- Complements AGAFA governance themes around traceability, reproducibility, and structured knowledge exchange.
