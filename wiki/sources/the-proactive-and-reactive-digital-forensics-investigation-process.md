Systematic literature review (SLR)-derived process model that integrates proactive live-forensics automation with traditional reactive post-incident investigation.

## Citation
Alharbi, S., Weber-Jahnke, J., & Traore, I. (2011). *The Proactive and Reactive Digital Forensics Investigation Process: A Systematic Literature Review*. In ISA 2011, CCIS 200, pp. 87-100.

## Key points
- Reviews digital forensics investigation process literature (2001-2010) to identify phase patterns and gaps.
- Finds that most published processes are reactive-only; only one prior model explicitly includes a proactive/live component.
- Proposes a two-component functional process: **proactive** then **reactive**, with decision handoff between them.
- Defines proactive phases as automated **proactive collection**, **event triggering**, **proactive preservation**, **proactive analysis**, and **preliminary report**.
- Retains reactive post-mortem phases grounded in traditional DF process models (identification, preservation, collection, analysis, final report).
- Frames anti-forensics pressure (for example, evidence hiding/overwriting) as a key driver for proactive evidence handling and automation.
- Emphasizes forensic soundness and evidence integrity during proactive operations so resulting evidence remains prosecution-usable.

## Scope note
This source proposes a conceptual/functional process and automation goals; it is not a full implementation standard and predates more recent operational guidance.
