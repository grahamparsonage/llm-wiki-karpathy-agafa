Systematic literature/software review of OWL reasoners and related systems assessing practical usability and maintenance status in 2023.

## Citation

Abicht, K. (2023). OWL Reasoners still useable in 2023. arXiv:2309.06888v1.

## Problem and aim

OWL reasoners remain central to ontology-driven workflows, but many widely cited tools appear inactive or difficult to deploy. The paper aims to provide an objective, large-scale usability/maintenance landscape of available reasoners and reasoner-based systems.

## Method snapshot

- Combined literature and software survey (May-July 2023).
- Reviewed over 100 entries total, including standalone reasoners and systems using external reasoners.
- Collected metadata on availability, startup/usability, maintenance activity, repositories, and documentation.
- Published raw research dataset for reuse.

## Key findings

- Substantial gap exists between historically prominent OWL reasoners and currently maintainable/usable tool choices.
- Many projects had weak maintenance signals, missing artifacts, or uncertain deployability.
- Final inventory included broad coverage, but only a subset met both usability and active-maintenance criteria.
- Reinforces that implementation decisions should include software-lifecycle and operational factors, not only formal reasoning capability.

## Relevance to this wiki

- Adds an implementation-readiness lens for ontology stacks that depend on OWL reasoning infrastructure.
- Supports practical risk assessment in architecture choices where reasoning engines are part of validation/governance pipelines.
- Complements existing conceptual ontology pages with software-maintenance evidence.
