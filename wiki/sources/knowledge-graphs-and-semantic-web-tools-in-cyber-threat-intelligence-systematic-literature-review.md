Systematic literature review of ontology and knowledge-graph use in cyber threat intelligence (CTI), including construction, utilization, and ML/DL-assisted enrichment.

## Citation

Bratsas, C., Anastasiadis, E. K., Angelidis, A. K., Ioannidis, L., Kotsakis, R., & Ougiaroglou, S. (2024). Knowledge Graphs and Semantic Web Tools in Cyber Threat Intelligence: A Systematic Literature Review. *Journal of Cybersecurity and Privacy*, 4, 518-545. https://doi.org/10.3390/jcp4030025

## Problem and aim

CTI data is heterogeneous and distributed across many formats and sources, which complicates interoperability and analysis. The paper reviews how semantic-web methods (ontologies and KGs) are being used to structure CTI and improve inference, sharing, and automation.

## Method snapshot

- Follows PRISMA-style screening.
- Searches multiple major databases across 2013 to early 2024.
- Final corpus: 76 studies.
- Organizes findings into ontology/KG construction, utilization, and ML/DL-enabled creation or extension.

## Key findings

- Ontology and KG approaches are increasingly used in CTI for normalization, correlation, and richer cross-source reasoning.
- STIX remains a key representation baseline but semantic layers are used to increase expressiveness and interoperability.
- Multiple studies connect CTI ontologies to digital-forensics representation efforts, including DFAX/CASE/UCO-related lines.
- ML/DL integration is most useful when it extends or enriches graph/ontology structures rather than remaining isolated extraction-only pipelines.

## Relevance to this wiki

- Provides a broad state-of-the-art map connecting CTI semantics with digital-forensics ontology work already tracked in the wiki.
- Adds evidence for implementation-readiness discussions around UCO/CASE/DFAX ecosystems.
- Supports future analysis pages on practical ontology-stack selection and maintenance.
