Review-of-reviews consolidating digital forensics research trajectories, recurring methodological gaps, and high-priority future directions.

## Citation
Casino, F., Dasaklis, T. K., Spathoulas, G. P., Anagnostopoulos, M., Ghosal, A., Borocz, I., Solanas, A., Conti, M., & Patsakis, C. (2022). *Research trends, challenges, and emerging topics in digital forensics: A review of reviews*. IEEE Access, 10, 25464-25493. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2022.3154059

## Key points
- Aggregates prior review papers to provide a high-level map of dominant DF subfields and publication trends.
- Surfaces recurring cross-cutting challenges: heterogeneity of evidence sources, tooling fragmentation, skills scarcity, and legal or jurisdictional friction.
- Emphasises reproducibility and benchmark quality gaps that reduce comparability of proposed techniques.
- Frames emerging priorities including cloud, mobile, and IoT forensics, AI-assisted workflows, and automation governance.

## Scope note
This source is captured as a review-oriented evidence map; conclusions should be interpreted in context of each paper's methodology and evaluation depth.
