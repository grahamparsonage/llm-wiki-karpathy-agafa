Canonical cyber security body-of-knowledge reference (v1.1.0) that structures the discipline into top-level categories and KAs, including a dedicated `Forensics` KA relevant to this wiki.

## Citation
CyBOK Editorial Team. (2021, July 31). *The Cyber Security Body of Knowledge (CyBOK), Version 1.1.0*. National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), UK. https://www.cybok.org/

## Key points
- Positions CyBOK as a community-built, openly licensed knowledge baseline for cyber security education, training, professional development, and programme mapping.
- Organises the domain into KAs grouped under top-level categories, supporting traceable curriculum and certification alignment.
- Includes dedicated KAs for `Security Operations & Incident Management` and `Forensics`, with explicit treatment of legal concerns, conceptual models, acquisition, and analysis.
- Describes a governance and change-management model for periodic updates, peer review, and public consultation.
- Notes v1.1.0 updates including additional KAs and major revision work in Network Security.

## Forensics-relevant outline highlights
- `8 Security Operations & Incident Management` includes workflow concepts, monitoring data sources, analysis methods, SIEM planning, and response operations.
- `9 Forensics` includes definitions and conceptual models, legal concerns (including Daubert framing), acquisition/analysis workflow distinctions, operating system and storage forensics, and data recovery/carving topics.

## Scope note
CyBOK is a knowledge-structuring and curriculum-mapping reference, not a procedural standard. It is strongest for taxonomy, competency framing, and curriculum/profession alignment rather than prescribing case-level forensic SOPs.
