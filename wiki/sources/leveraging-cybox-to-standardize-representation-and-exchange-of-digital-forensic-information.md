DFRWS paper proposing CybOX as a foundation for structured forensic information exchange and introducing DFAX as a domain-specific extension for forensic actions and provenance.

## Citation

Casey, E., Back, G., & Barnum, S. (2015). Leveraging CybOX to standardize representation and exchange of digital forensic information. *Digital Investigation*, 12(Supplement 1), S102-S110. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.diin.2015.01.014

## Problem and aim

Digital-forensics outputs are often ad hoc, tool-specific, and difficult to fuse across workflows and jurisdictions. The paper aims to standardize representation/exchange to improve correlation, verification, provenance tracking, and machine-to-machine interoperability.

## Core contributions

- Evaluates strengths and limitations of existing schemas for forensic information sharing.
- Demonstrates use of CybOX for technical object/relationship representation and provenance capture.
- Proposes Digital Forensic Analysis eXpression (DFAX) as an additional open schema/ontology layer for forensic-domain semantics.
- Introduces early UCO-aligned abstractions for broader cross-domain consistency.

## Key implications

- Better support for combining multi-tool evidence outputs (timeline reconstruction, link analysis, dual-tool comparison).
- Stronger provenance/accountability structure for trust and authenticity.
- Clear bridge from technical observables to higher-level forensic actions and investigative context.

## Relevance to this wiki
