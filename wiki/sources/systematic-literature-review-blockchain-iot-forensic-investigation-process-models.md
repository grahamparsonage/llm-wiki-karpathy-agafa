SLR mapping blockchain-enabled IoT forensic process models, recurring workflow phases, and adoption constraints in evidence handling.

## Citation
Akinbi, A., MacDermott, A., & Ismael, A. M. (2022). *A systematic literature review of blockchain-based Internet of Things (IoT) forensic investigation process models*. Forensic Science International: Digital Investigation, 43, 301444. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fsidi.2022.301444

## Key points
- Synthesises reported blockchain-IoT forensic process models and compares their phase structure against conventional DF workflows.
- Highlights chain-of-custody, integrity, and traceability motivations for adopting blockchain in IoT evidence pipelines.
- Identifies implementation barriers including scalability, interoperability, legal admissibility concerns, and skills gaps.
- Shows most studies remain architecture/prototype-heavy with limited operational validation in real investigative settings.
