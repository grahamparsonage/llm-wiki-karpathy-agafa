State-of-the-art review of machine-learning use in smart-environment digital forensic investigations, with method families, opportunities, and constraints.

## Citation
Tageldin, L., & Venter, H. (2023). *Machine-learning forensics: State of the art in the use of machine-learning techniques for digital forensic investigations within smart environments*. Applied Sciences, 13(18), 10169. https://doi.org/10.3390/app131810169

## Key points
- Reviews machine-learning approaches used for evidence discovery, triage, anomaly detection, and attribution in smart environments.
- Categorises practical DF application contexts across IoT, smart-home, and connected-system evidence ecosystems.
- Identifies key limitations around dataset quality, explainability, reproducibility, and operational transferability.
- Reinforces need for hybrid human-plus-automation workflows rather than fully autonomous forensic decision-making.
