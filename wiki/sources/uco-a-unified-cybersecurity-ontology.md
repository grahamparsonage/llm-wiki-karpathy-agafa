Conference workshop paper introducing UCO as a core cybersecurity ontology for integrating heterogeneous cyber data, standards, and linked open data sources.

## Citation

Syed, Z., Padia, A., Finin, T., Mathews, L., & Joshi, A. (2016). UCO: A Unified Cybersecurity Ontology. In *Proceedings of the AAAI Workshop on Artificial Intelligence for Cyber Security (WS-16-03)*, 195-202.

## Problem and aim

Cybersecurity information is fragmented across tools, formats, and standards. The paper proposes UCO to provide a shared semantic backbone for integration, reasoning, and broader situational awareness.

## Core design points

- Extends earlier IDS ontology work and maps to commonly used cybersecurity standards and schemas.
- Represents entities and relations in RDF/OWL to support machine-understandable integration and inference.
- Connects cybersecurity concepts to external linked open data sources for richer context and broader use cases.
- Positions UCO as a hub ontology, similar to how DBpedia serves as a general linked-data core.

## Example value

- Supports correlation of vulnerabilities, products, vendors, and incident context across internal and external data.
- Enables query patterns that combine cyber evidence with non-cyber knowledge graphs.
- Improves interoperability and reusable rule authoring in multi-source security workflows.

## Relevance to this wiki

- Directly relevant to AGAFA ontology grounding and interoperability goals.
- Provides historical context for UCO/CASE lineage used in later smart-city and forensic extensions.
- Reinforces knowledge-graph-first reasoning patterns that complement retrieval and rule validation.
