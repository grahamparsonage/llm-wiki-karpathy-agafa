UK-focused policy briefing mapping the legal and procedural framework for digital forensic and social-media evidence from collection through disclosure and court presentation.

## Citation
Goldstraw-White, J. (2022, September). *Legal and policy framework for digital forensics: A resource for practitioners*. Digital Forensics and Social Media project briefing (PRCI, UCL, and ICPR/Birkbeck).

## Key points
- Frames digital evidence governance as a lifecycle from identification and access through extraction, preservation, transfer, analysis, disclosure, and courtroom use.
- Highlights that digital evidence handling in serious crime can engage a wide set of overlapping UK statutes and procedural obligations rather than one single framework.
- Emphasises privacy, proportionality, and lawful authority as cross-cutting constraints throughout evidence collection and review activity.
- Notes operational challenge from data scale, device diversity, and cross-jurisdictional data location, with implications for admissibility and disclosure practice.
- References 2022 legislative updates (including Police, Crime, Sentencing and Courts Act device-extraction provisions) as part of the evolving UK legal landscape.

## Scope note
This source is a UK policy/practice briefing rather than a method-validation standard. It is most useful for legal-procedural framing, scoping, and governance design.
