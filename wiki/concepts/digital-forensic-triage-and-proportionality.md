A UK-focused approach to prioritizing digital forensic activity based on investigative value, legal scope, and resource constraints.

## Definition
Triage in digital forensics is a process for prioritizing evidence actions; it is not equivalent to using a single triage tool.

## Core ideas
- Scope and focus should be justified and documented.
- Seizure/examination should be proportionate to investigative need.
- Digital devices should not be seized by default; reasons should be explicit.
- Investigators and forensic practitioners should regularly align on strategy.

## Why it matters
Proportionate triage improves timeliness while preserving defensibility and reducing unnecessary intrusion.

## In practice
- Define evidential questions before broad extraction.
- Select evidence sources strategically (device, provider records, online source, in-transit data).
- Update strategy as case direction changes.

## Scope note
UK-focused framing; legal thresholds and procedures vary by jurisdiction.
