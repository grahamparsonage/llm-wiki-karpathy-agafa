Community-maintained cyber security knowledge framework that structures the field into defined Knowledge Areas (KAs) for education, training, certification mapping, and professional capability development.

## Definition
CyBOK is a curated reference model of cyber security knowledge designed to provide shared terminology and topic boundaries across academia, training providers, employers, and professional bodies.

## Why it matters in this wiki
- Provides a recognised external taxonomy for positioning digital forensics topics relative to adjacent areas (for example operations, law/regulation, cryptography, and systems security).
- Supports clearer capability mapping from conceptual pages to training pathways and role expectations.
- Helps distinguish between knowledge-structure references and operational standards/guidance.

## Forensics-relevant elements
- Dedicated `Forensics` KA with conceptual models, legal concerns, data acquisition/analysis distinctions, and system/storage forensic topics.
- Close coupling with `Security Operations & Incident Management` KA for monitor-analyse-plan-respond workflows and incident-to-forensics handoff.

## Limitations
- CyBOK does not replace jurisdiction-specific legal requirements, method-validation guidance, or laboratory SOPs.
- It defines knowledge scope; it does not itself certify tool reliability or evidential admissibility.
