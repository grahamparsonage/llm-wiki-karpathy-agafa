The ACPO four principles define core safeguards for handling digital evidence in UK-focused investigative contexts.

## Principles
1. No action should change data that may be relied upon in court.
2. If original data must be accessed, the person must be competent and able to explain relevance/implications.
3. An audit trail of all processes should be created and preserved so an independent third party can reproduce the result.
4. The person in charge has overall responsibility for legal and principle compliance.

## Why it matters
These principles provide practical guardrails for evidence integrity, defensibility, and reproducibility in digital investigations.

## Practical implications
- Prefer imaging/copy-first workflows where practicable.
- Record all actions and rationale, including scope decisions.
- Ensure role-appropriate competence before intrusive access.
- Maintain accountability at investigation leadership level.

## Scope note
This concept is UK-focused and should not be treated as universal legal advice.
