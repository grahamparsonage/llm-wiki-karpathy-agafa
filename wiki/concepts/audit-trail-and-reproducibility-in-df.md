Digital forensic processes should be recorded so an independent reviewer can examine the process and reproduce outcomes.

## Definition
Audit trail and reproducibility refer to maintaining complete records of forensic actions, decisions, and transformations applied to digital evidence.

## Key requirements
- Keep contemporaneous notes of actions taken.
- Preserve process records sufficient for independent review.
- Record rationale for scope, triage, and examination decisions.
- Distinguish factual findings from interpretive opinion in reporting.

## Why it matters
Reproducibility strengthens evidential trust, supports disclosure obligations, and reduces risk of misinterpretation.

## Typical records
- Scene actions and handling notes
- Imaging/acquisition details
- Tool/process steps
- Communication and decision records
- Reporting assumptions and limitations

## Scope note
This page captures UK-focused guidance context from ACPO; equivalent requirements differ across jurisdictions.
