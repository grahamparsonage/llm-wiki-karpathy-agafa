Structured demonstration that an end-to-end digital forensic method is fit for its intended use, with documented evidence, explicit limits, and ongoing control after deployment.

## Definition
Method validation in digital forensics is the process of producing objective evidence that a method (including tools, process steps, and practitioner interaction) is suitable for a specific end-user requirement and can be relied on in investigative and court-facing contexts.

## Core stages
- **End-user requirement:** Define what outcomes must be reliable and for whom (including court-facing use).
- **Method definition:** Document the method as an SOP with quality controls and risk mitigations.
- **Risk assessment:** Identify realistic failure modes and their justice impact.
- **Validation plan:** Specify tests, data sources, expected outcomes, tolerances, and limitations.
- **Execution and evaluation:** Record contemporaneous test evidence and assess pass/fail against acceptance criteria.
- **Closure and implementation:** Issue validation report and completion statement, then deploy with training and ongoing QA.

## Validation scale patterns
- **Novel method:** Requires broader developmental validation and more extensive objective evidence.
- **Adopted/adapted method:** Can rely partly on external validation data, but requires critical review and local verification of competence.
- **Minor change:** Needs risk-based impact assessment and targeted testing; full re-validation may still be necessary when change is significant.

## Common failure modes
- Treating tool checks as equivalent to method validation.
- Relying only on case-by-case QA (for example peer review or dual-tool checks) without validating underlying method reliability.
- Deferring validation work until audit deadlines rather than maintaining continuous method governance.

## Why it matters
Method validation links technical reliability, uncertainty disclosure, practitioner competence, and auditability into a single defensible assurance case for forensic reporting.
