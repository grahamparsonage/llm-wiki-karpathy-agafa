Writing rule for digital forensic outputs: clearly separate observed facts from interpretive opinions and state limitations explicitly.

## Rule
Always distinguish:
- **Fact**: what was observed/recovered and how.
- **Opinion**: interpretation, inference, or conclusion drawn from facts.

## Application guidance
- Label opinion statements explicitly.
- State the evidential basis for each opinion.
- Describe limitations and uncertainty clearly.
- Use plain language suitable for non-specialist readers where possible.

## Do
- "Recovered file metadata shows timestamp X." (fact)
- "Based on metadata and usage artifacts, it is likely that..." (opinion)

## Avoid
- Blending interpretation into factual narrative without labeling.
- Overstating certainty where technical ambiguity exists.
- Omitting caveats about process or data limitations.

## Scope note
UK-focused guidance context; adapt report formality and disclosure wording to local legal procedure when needed.
